Overview

We approached the issue of auditing algorithmic bias through social proof to motivate and improve confidence of end-users in the auditing process. Through our research, we discovered 3 overarching insights: end-user’s desire for simplicity, transparency, and anonymity.

We proposed a holistic solution that integrates a chatbot into Instagram’s user interface—AuditBot—to help social media end-users assess and audit potentially biased posts, enabling the co-creation of value between Instagram and its end-users.

Goals

  1. Simplicity. Create an easily discovered and traversed auditing process.

  2. Transparency. Facilitate communication between end-users and the platform.

  3. Anonymity. Ensure that end-users have validation and privacy in their audits.

Research

Background Research • Data Analysis

Explored the topic of auditing algorithmic bias and potential areas of intervention.

Background Research

We focused on utilizing informational and experiental methods to explore the problem space.

Through researching 12 data sources, we found that algorithmic bias has roots in the people that are involved in developing the algorithm (intrinsic and extrinsic bias), the data used to create the algorithms (confirmation bias), and how algorithms approach problems.

Data Analysis

We used word clouds and sentiment analyses to understand how current end users express their thoughts about instances of algorithmic bias, and interpreted:

  • User sentiment is primarily focused on negative actions.

  • Social factors, such as culture and language, impact the variance of user feedback.

Define

Project Scope  •  Generative Think-Aloud  •  Contextual Inquiry & Interpretation

Focused research efforts towards social-proof and motivation-driven interventions to improve end-user engagement in auditing algorithmic bias through multiple platforms.

Project Scope

From our preliminary research, we decided to focus on interventions involving social-proof to motivate and increase confidence in end-users to report instance of algorithmic bias.

We were particularly interested in what would motivate end-users to report instances of algorithmic bias.

Generative Think-Aloud

We tested with 5 participants on Youtube and thematically analyzed participant feedback to quickly gain understanding of how everyday users perceive the impact of reporting algorithmic bias. 

We found that users feel unheard by platforms and gravitate towards simple and immediate actions when confronted with explicit biases, such as ignoring it entirely or simply using a reaction to show their thoughts.

From this study, we made sure to consider providing a sentiment of validation and integrating auditing capabilities into our chosen solution and platform. 

Users have difficulty recognizing bias and gravitate towards simple and immediate actions.

Contextual Inquiry & Interpretation

With a think-aloud following personal social media, we tested 5 participants and conducted 5 interpretation sessions to understand how social proof impacts users’ motivation to act. 

We found that participants’ relationships encourage interaction with certain social media content, and that the level of interaction depends on each relationship.

Further, participants were aware of algorithms, and actively manipulated them to tailor their experience with the platform.

From this study, we understood that increased interaction with content by users related to the participants would result in potentially higher levels of interaction by the participant.

Ideate

Storyboarding • Speed Dating

Developed multiple avenues of interventions supported by our research and quickly captured user reactions.

Storyboarding

Created 30 storyboards based off of prior research that would meet end-user needs, and refine 15 to show to users in speed dating.

Speed Dating

We quickly evaluated ideas with 5 users to determine end-user desires in proposed interventions (from storyboarding).

Insights:

  1. Users prefer anonymity when reporting bias or harmful content.

  2. Social proof motivates users to act on biased/harmful content, but only with a certain level of anonymity.

  3. Users prefer intrinsic rewards over extrinsic rewards when reporting bias.

Design

Product-Service Design • Additional Design Considerations • Presentation

Developed AuditBot (in Instagram) as an intervention that met user needs of simplicity, transparency, and anonymity.

Product-Service Design

Analyzed user research and insights to develop AuditBot to meet end-user needs.

We decided to use Instagram as a platform to design with, as it had:

  • Sufficient content (potential for algorithmic bias).
  • Opportunity to incorporate an interactive agent to guide the user through the auditing process.
  • Design patterns we could use as inspiration for the user interface.

Accounted for Instagram’s business needs and sought to co-create value by way of increasing end-user and platform communication and leveraging end-user relationships.

We also took inspiration from Instagram’s mode to hide likes from end-users when anonymizing user information. Instead of showing who reported the post, it would be a number of relationships the user had.

Presentation

We communicated AuditBot’s unique value proposition at a remote poster session to over 30 attendees.
