CAPSEA

Project Lead and Consultant - CMU Consulting Team

Role: Project Lead, Consultant.

Team: Meredith Levene (Co-Project Lead), Frank Zhou, Jialu Xiang.

Deliverables: Proposal, Current State Assessment, Subject Matter Expert (SME) Interviews, Business and Recommendation Plan, Training and Implementation Plan, Presentation.

Client: Citizens Against Physical, Sexual, and Emotional Abuse (CAPSEA) Inc. (501(c)3 designated non-profit)

Timeline: September - December 2021.

Location: Pittsburgh, PA.

Overview

We collaborated with JoAnne Weyant, Executive Director of CAPSEA to improve the organization’s collection and usage of confidential client information and provide recommendations for digital transformation.

Through our current state assessment, we found pain points in the organization’s communication in day-to-day operations and opportunities for improvement in their data collection process.

We developed recommendations and a training plan to improve CAPSEA’s data collection and communication process with the intention of increasing productivity, ability to process clients, and ease of submission of data for future funding.

We proposed an improved data communication process in the organization along with a recommendation to adopt a HIPAA compliant Google Workspace and a training plan.

Goals

  1. Determine a platform for data collection and communication.

  2. Improve the processing of clients.

  3. Generate a business and training plan.

Considerations

  • HIPAA compliance and confidential client data.

  • Technical ability of employees and willingness to adopt new technologies.

  • Financial constraints on potential solutions.

Background

Our client, Citizens Against Physical, Sexual, and Emotional Abuse (CAPSEA), experienced an influx of calls from users in Elk County and Cameron County during the COVID-19 pandemic.

As a non-profit, they primarily are funded by the federal government and need to report instances in which they assist individuals through a secure service.

Presenting Problem

Our client’s issue was presented as an issue with data throughout the entire organization—the collection of data, the usage of data, and most importantly, the reporting of data to funding sources.

Upon further conversations with our client, we found that their current data collection and usage process was based on hard copies of data—mainly through paper notes—as they were concerned that the use of digital software would violate the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and endanger individuals seeking assistance from their organization.

Project Goals

CAPSEA identified two areas where additional support would be needed: data collection of user information, and their call information and handling.

To help CAPSEA shape an efficient and accurate data collection system, we plan to accomplish the following goals:

  • Improve current process of accepting and processing clients.

  • Determine the most appropriate data platform tailored for CAPSEA’s needs including confidentiality, accuracy, and affordability.

  • Generate a business plan, training manual, and budget for plan implementation.

Method

To fulfill our goals and CAPSEA’s goals for the project, we approached the project through 3 major phases:

  • Investigation of data infrastructure and client processing: a report of the current state of CAPSEA data collection (interviews, current process).

  • Recommended data infrastructure and client processing: a report on potential data collection platforms and procedures (market research, new process).

  • Compilation of findings: recommendations, implementation plan, training plan, and budget.

Evaluation Criteria

To measure our team’s success in the project, we set the following criteria:

Data Infrastructure

  • Addressing both security and confidentiality concerns via encrypted data storage.
  • Allowing all CAPSEA staff to easily access client information simultaneously.
  • Ensuring data accuracy and integrity.
Client Processing
  • Reducing time on task for gathering information from clients and transferring that information to the Efforts to Outcomes (ETO) software.
  • Tracking calls to be able to follow-up with clients.
  • Enabling the collection of call analytics to better serve clients.
Compilation of Findings
  • Elucidating the exact steps and contacts needed to fulfill the first two objectives.
  • Developing thorough training to ensure that integration will occur smoothly.
  • Creating a budget that will allow for technological advancement and be financially feasible for CAPSEA to sustain.

Literature Review

Intentions

We evaluated 12 sources from academic journals and research to understand how we could help CAPSEA:

  1. Establish more streamlined internal communication.
  2. Improve productivity (with regards to data collection and use).
  3. Execute change management to establish a new data collection and usage process.

 

Insights

From our literature review, we developed the following insights on potential client actions:

  • Establish a great sense of urgency to adopt the new system.
  • Remove obstacles for employees and push benefits to their daily workflow.
  • Dedicate sufficient resources to acquire and train the employees.
  • Communicate relevant information.
  • Pay attention to the emotions arising from change (empower employees to express tension and anxiety about the change).

Subject Matter Expert (SME) Insights

Interviews

To help contextualize our findings, we also reached out to the 3 subject-matter experts:

  • Jordan Pallitto, COO and Management Consultant at the Hill Group.
  • David Lassman, Distinguished Service Professor of Organizational Management at Carnegie Mellon University.
  • Sunkee Lee, Assistant Professor of Organizational Theory and Strategy at Carnegie Mellon University.

 

Insights

From our interviews, we found that:

  1. Scoping our team’s intervention to a process redesign and technology change management would enhance our focus on where CAPSEA’s communication was breaking down.
  2. Discussions about key data and standardized data facilitates better data collection and usage.
  3. Employee understanding of interrelated work helps facilitate internal communication.
  4. Training programs need to be systematic—a standard set of rules and process that are easy to follow.

Staff Insights

Our team met with our client, as well as an additional 6 employees from CAPSEA to understand how the data processing issue affected their day-to-day, their ideal process for entering and using data, and their technology fluency.

From our interviews, we found that CAPSEA staff:

  • Are unable to access the central data software used to store and report data for the organization (due to pricing and security access limitations).

  • Fill out individuals' data on paper, and then provide it to another staff member to enter it into the system manually.

  • Are only able to digitally communicate oversimplified and unidentifiable data to protect individuals.

  • Do not have dedicated personnel to focus on data and technology due to lack of resources and organization size.

  • Have errors in data collection are difficult to fix, as data is only processed periodically through a single individual.

  • Do not have a direct line of communication between different departments—it is usually done through their weekly all hands meeting or a 1:1 conversation.

  • Already are familiar with Google Workspace to store blank copies of documents and to send important updates.

Client Considerations

From our insights on CAPSEA’s current data process, we needed to consider the following for our recommendation:

Cost: aligning with the organization’s budget for the year and towards the future, as data storage would most likely entail a recurring cost.

Technical capabilities matching our recommendation to the level of technical expertise the organization maintained from leadership to employees, as well as the lack of IT personnel in CAPSEA.

Ease of use: ensuring a solution with high levels of support, abundant resources for troubleshooting, and the ability to implement and learn quickly.

Future potential: providing a platform that allows for easy expansion and customization to meet the growing needs of CAPSEA through the future expansion plans.

Pain Points

From our diagram of CAPSEA’s current data process, we identified x major pain points we wanted to rectify with our recommendation:

  • Data infrastructure that is HIPAA compliant.

  • Accurate, timely data to be submitted to CAPSEA’s major funding source (federal).

  • Minimize time needed to communicate data amongst the organization.

Technology Solution • New Data Process

Technology Solution

After considering potential solutions that would meet the pain points we discovered, our team decided to recommend a new workflow using Google Workspace (specifically for non-profits).

We found that Google Workspace not only had the ability to be HIPAA compliant (as of November 2021), but also required relatively little technology maintenance and had a minimal cost.

We decided against industry standards for data storage such as Amazon Web Services due to the lack of IT personnel in the organization and the time it would take to implement it in CAPSEA.

New Data Process

From our technology solution, we considered a new workflow that would meet the stakeholder needs.

In particular, we focused on redesigning internal processes for handling callers, using Google Forms, Google Docs, Google Sheets, and Gmail to digitize CAPSEA’s data collection and communication of information when necessary.

Deliver

Supporting Documents • Recommendation • Presentation

Supporting Documents

To share our insights and recommendations to CAPSEA in-depth, we created a business plan, training manual, and budget to:

  • Provide advice (from our research and SME interviews) for how to manage the transition in technology.

  • Display measurable, achievable, and actionable steps for implementing the technology solution and beginning training.

  • Reinforce the need for our recommendation and justify the cost.

Recommendation

For reference, our initial project goals:

  1. Determine the most appropriate data platform tailored for CAPSEA’s needs including confidentiality, accuracy, and affordability.
  2. Improve current process of accepting and processing clients.
  3. Generate a business plan, training manual, and budget for plan implementation.
Our recommendation—the use of Google Workspace and a new work process flow—met our initial project goals.
  • Google Workspace had HIPAA compliance capabilities, allowed for detailed data collection in Google Forms, and was affordable for CAPSEA.

  • Our new workflow would digitize CAPSEA’s operations for receiving calls and allow easier dissemination of information when needed between departments.

  • Our business plan, training manual, and budget (as well as prior reports) covered our process and final recommendation in-depth.

Presentation

After handing off our documentation and holding a final meeting with our client, we presented our process, findings, and final recommendation to over 30 individuals.

Next Steps

Reflection • Outcomes

Reflection

Given more time, I would have:

  • Met CAPSEA employees in-person to thoroughly understand their day-to-day and map out interdependent tasks.

  • Taken time to go step-by-step through CAPSEA’s call process and relationship between departments to see common use cases.

  • Understand the perspective of individuals seeking assistance (due to privacy and confidentiality reasons, we did not seek to speak with those individuals during our project).

  • Quantified the impact of digitized, prompt, and accurate data collection for the organization at a high level and at the individual level.

Outcomes

From this project, I learned the following:

  • Small efficiencies—minimizing time to communicate and share data—are compounded when used across an entire organization.

  • Motivation at the organizational level and at the individual level are needed to help bring about change.

  • Communication with stakeholders in the perspective of their role helps provide a clear, detailed picture of their interactions and focus day-to-day.
