Reflection • Outcomes

Reflection

As an aspiring product manager, I learned the impact of strong leadership at the organizational and individual level. From this experience, I was able to see how product owners and product managers motivate and drive the progress of large teams, as well as how individuals in more defined roles took leadership of their work to share out with the larger team.

On the other hand, I was able to practice prioritization in my project and in helping plan for future features—understanding how to maximize development hours and minimize time spent on “black holes” in favor of the greater vision of the product.

Outcomes

Although I am unable to share the nature of my work publicly, I was able to fully experience the day-to-day of a product team during my time at J.P. Morgan Chase & Co. I not only gained practical knowledge about the inner-workings of SCRUM teams, but I also had the opportunity to see the process of taking insights and pain points from users to determining features for the product.

Further, the experience I gained in disseminating complex technical information to non-technical audiences is something I will take to future teams, along with cross-discipline communication between business, product, design, and technology.
